3. Who contributes the most per appearance?

This is all about efficiency, which players make the biggest impact every time they step on the pitch. To reach a conclusion, we created a Goal Involvement stat by adding goals and assists, then divided it by appearances to see who packs the most punch per game. Filtering out players with fewer than five appearances keeps the sample fair.

premleague <- premleague %>%
  mutate(GoalInvolvement = Goals + Assists,
         InvolvementPerAppearance = GoalInvolvement / Appearances)

top10efficiency <- premleague %>%
  filter(Appearances > 5) %>%
  arrange(-InvolvementPerAppearance) %>%
  slice_head(n = 10)

ggplot(top10efficiency, aes(x = InvolvementPerAppearance, 
                             y = reorder(Name, InvolvementPerAppearance))) +
  geom_segment(aes(x = 0, 
                   xend = InvolvementPerAppearance, 
                   yend = Name), 
               color = "grey") +
  geom_point(color = "darkblue", size = 4) +
  geom_text(aes(label = round(InvolvementPerAppearance, 2)), 
            vjust = -0.8, size = 3.5) +
  labs(
    title = "Top 10 Players by Goal Involvement per Appearance",
    x = "Goal Involvement per Appearance",
    y = "Player"
  ) +
  theme_classic()

From the results, you can spot the real game changers. The players who arent just scoring or assisting occasionally, theyre delivering consistently every time they play. Its like a cheat code for impact, the longer the line and the higher the dot, the more indispensable the player. Basically, these top 10 are the per-appearance legends of the league, showing who you want on your fantasy team if you care about efficiency over volume.

6. What percentage of a teams goals does each player contribute?

Here, we dive into who really carries their teams attack. First, we calculated each clubs total goals, then determined what percentage of that total each player scored. By arranging players from highest to lowest contribution and picking the top 15, we can see the true goal-getters.

teamgoals <- premleague %>%
  group_by(Club) %>%
  summarise(teamtotalgoals = sum(Goals, na.rm = TRUE))

playersharedgoal <- premleague %>%
  left_join(teamgoals, by = "Club") %>%
  mutate(
    sharedgoal = (Goals / teamtotalgoals) * 100
  )

topcontributors <- playersharedgoal %>%
  arrange(-sharedgoal) %>%
  slice_head(n = 15)

ggplot(topcontributors, aes(
  x = reorder(Name, sharedgoal),
  y = sharedgoal,
  fill = Club
)) +
  geom_col() +
  coord_flip() +
  labs(
    title = "Percentage of Team Goals Contributed by Players",
    x = "Player",
    y = "Percent of Team Goals"
  ) +
  theme_minimal()

The results show that indeed there is an I in team. From the results, you can identify the players who are mission-critical for their squads, those who, if they dont score, their teams attack really struggles. This visualization turns raw goal counts into a perspective on influence and responsibility, showing who is the true heartbeat of their teams offense.

What four players would you build a dream team around?

library(knitr)

dreamteam <- premleague %>%
  mutate(Position = as.character(Position)) %>%
  
  group_by(Position) %>%
  slice_max(
    order_by = case_when(
      Position == "Forward"     ~ Goals,
      Position == "Midfielder"  ~ Assists,
      Position == "Defender"    ~ Tackles,
      Position == "Goalkeeper"  ~ Clean.sheets,
    ),
    n = 1
  ) %>%
  ungroup() %>%
  
  select(Name, Club, Position, Goals, Assists, Tackles, Clean.sheets)

kable(dreamteam, caption = "Premier League Dream Team")
